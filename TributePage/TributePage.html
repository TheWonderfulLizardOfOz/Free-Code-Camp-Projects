Alan Turing

The father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence

  • Born: 23/06/1912
  • 1934: Turing graduated University of cambridge
  • 1936: Turing’s seminal paper “On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem" was published
  • 1939: Turing moved to Bletchley park
  • 1940: Turing and other cryptanalysts developed a code breaking machine known as the Bombe which was used to decrypt intercepted messages during the war
  • 1942: Turing developed the an algorithm that could decrypt messages engrypted by a German ciphering machine known as the "tunny"
  • 1945: Turing recruited to National Phhysical Laboratory
  • 1951: Turing elected as a fellow of the royal society of London
  • 1952: Turing convicted of "gross indecency" - at the time homosexuality was illegal
  • 1952: Turing published “The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis”
  • Died: 07/06/1954
  • Official verdict of Turing's death was suicide, the most common reason for suicide is attributed to the hormone treatment he recieved. According to the records surrounding the inquest of Turing's death there was no evidence that he intended to take his life.
  • 2013: Turing pardoned by Queen Elizabeth II
